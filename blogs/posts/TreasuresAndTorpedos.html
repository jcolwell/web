Treasures and Torpedoes

Programming and Development in Unity3D

Posted on Sept 4, 2015

In my third quarter at The Art Institute of Vancouver I had the opportunity to learn about programming and game development for Unity3D. During the 11 week course my classmates and I got the chance to see how Unity works and how to create a platformer and a shooter.

In the last four weeks or so of the course the class broke into groups and got to work to making a game. I had the pleasure of working with another programmer, Cole Rudniski as well as two game designers, Kevin Hill and Victoria Wojcik. And with that, Torpedo’s Treasures born!

…Ok well maybe not quite like that. First we planned what we wanted to do; a submarine game that the play could maneuver a 3D environment with the aim of collecting Davy Jones’ treasure. Ok that sounds like a start but is there anything else we can add? Enemy submarines to shoot down with torpedoes? Ah that sounds like an excellent idea!

The Player and the Claw

Now that we have an idea of what we want to create it was time to get to work. I was tasked with putting together the script that would allow the play to pilot their submarine and pick up the treasure. But one question; how to pick up the treasure?
